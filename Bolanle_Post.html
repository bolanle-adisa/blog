How my journey in the tech industry has been so far .

Hello everyone! I hope that my unconventional path into technology may encourage anyone else who is considering a career in this field. So here is my story!

My high school years

Prior to the start of my college education, I had no knowledge about all the perks of being in the tech industry. I attended high school in my home country, Nigeria, where we were given the liberty to decide what career path we wanted to take from an early age. There were three departments, the sciences, commercial and arts, from which I decided to study commercial courses because I intended on have an Accounting major. I graduated high school in July 2019 with a High school Diploma in commpercial courses like Accounting, Economics, Commerce, Banking and Finance and many more.

High school graduation
July 11th 2019

Life after high school

Due to the pandemic, I was not able to begin my college education immediately after graduation. During this time, I was privileged enough to join an advising center that enlightened me on the benefits having a Computer Science major could provide. This sparked my interest and I went on to do further research. I came across so many success stories of people with similar backgrounds as mine and I decided to start my journey, hopefully, I will become an inspiration to the younger generation like others were to me.

College struggles

When I started my coding classes at college, it was hard catching up because it was like learning a whole different language but this challenge did not hold me back. The fear of failure motivated me to utilize resources that I never knew existed. I discovered websites that taught me how to code in c++ and JAVA and in no time, I was excelling. Those resources ensured my success in my classes.
In addition to learning from online resources, I joined programs designed to improve and empower women of colour in the STEM industry. I go for conferences, participate in hackathons and get certifications that have provided me with resources to develop myself and discover what part of tech I was most interested in. I also currently work an on-campus job at my university as an IT technician. This job deals with computer hardware. I perform hardware and software installations, perform routine maintainance of computer hardware, attend to complaints made to the IT departments Help Desk, print Identification Cards for new and existing students and staff, perform routine updates on the universities PC's, amonst other things. I like the exposure this job has provided me because now I am able to know that I prefer dealing with computer software. I am also a Major League Hacking fellow; here, I am learning everyday to be a better open source citizen.

2022 NSBE conference
March 25th 2022

Summary

Not a lot of women were into tech in the past and I am glad that the percentage of women in tech is now rising. I have realized that no matter your background, all it takes is determination to attain success. Currently, I am gaining more experience by gaining internships that would expand my horizon. I am looking foward to growing, developing my skills and gaining new skills as I progress in life. Upon graduation with my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science and Mathematics, I hope to be able to be a part of the strong, successful women in tech and serve as an inspiration to the younger generation.


Thank you so much for taking the time to read my post and I hope it inspires you.
